Prestigious Schools

Technion-Israel Institute of Technology

Albert Einstein was the founding founder of the Technion, founded the first Technion-Israel Institute of Technology and served as its president. It is one of only 10 universities in the world to have built and launched an artificial satellite.

The Hebrew University of Jerusalem

The first university of the Jewish nation and a symbol of the cultural revival of the Jewish nation in its ancestral birthplace, and is a member of the Global Alliance of Advanced Studies. The University was founded in 1918 and completed in 1925.It ranked No. 1 in Israel, No. 5 in the Asia-Pacific region, and No. 87 in the world, with 15 Nobel laureates, two Fields Medalists, and three Turing Award winners among its alumni, professors, and researchers.

Tel Aviv University

Located in the heart of Israel’s high-tech, business and culture center, Tel Aviv University (TAU) represents the country at its best – dynamic, innovative, cosmopolitan and forward-looking.

University of Haifa

The largest research university in northern Israel, and the most pluralistic institution of higher education in the country. Founded in 1963.

Technion-Israel Institute of Technology

Founded in 1934, formerly known as the Sieveric Institute, expanded in 1949 and named after Dr. Haim Weizman, Israel's first president and renowned chemist. It is recognized as a research center for physics, chemistry, mathematics and life sciences.

Expert Lectures

Large Language Models: Current Capabilities, Future Applications, and Emerging Risks

Teaching Experts: Prof. Lev Muchnik

The purpose of this workshop is to discuss the latest developments in artificial intelligence, specifically the release of large language models such as ChatGPT and Bard. This workshop will explore the inner workings of the technology, its current capabilities, future expectations, and the risks associated with it.

Disruptive Innovation: The Many Forms of Innovation – Evolution VS Revolution

Teaching Experts: Prof. Yigal Newman

Innovation comes in many forms, and when technology is used to improve existing services or products, it can be revolutionary; When technology is used to come up with entirely new services, products, or extreme areas of business, it can be revolutionary. These lectures will introduce innovation using concrete examples of evolutionary and revolutionary innovations in today's fintech industry

Consumer credit and financial risk management

Teaching Experts: Prof. Dan Galai

First, the different nature of retail credit risk and commercial credit risk is introduced, and the main issues in credit risk assessment are highlighted. Second, we'll look at the process of credit scoring in more detail. Credit scoring is now a widely used technique, not only in the banking industry, but also in many other industries where there is a need to check a customer's credit status or estimate the likelihood of a customer making a claim. Finally, we will discuss innovations in retail banking and advancements in retail credit risk, including the use of big data and machine learning to better assess risk, and raise regulatory questions.
